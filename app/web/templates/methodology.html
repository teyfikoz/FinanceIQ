Methodology

Readable signals, not black-box promises.

FundPilot is designed as a public orientation layer. The objective is to compress noisy market inputs into a faster morning workflow, not to simulate a trading terminal or discretionary mandate.

Operating posture

Public

Read-only research surface

Server-side data fetches

No execution or custody

Data sources

Market cards use Yahoo Finance collectors for broad public market orientation.

Crypto cards and Fear & Greed sentiment use CoinGecko and related public inputs.

Liquidity and macro context use FRED when credentials are available, with clearly marked fallbacks otherwise.

Turkish fund workflows rely on TEFAS-derived server-side collection and cached peer-board ranking.

BIST catalyst workflows use KAP public company pages, public disclosure feeds, and notice PDFs when exact contract data can be parsed safely.

Public-surface limits

This site is not an OMS, PMS, or execution rail.

Signal scores are heuristics for orientation, not investment advice or suitability assessment.

Delayed, missing, or fallback data can appear during upstream outages; the UI now shows that state explicitly.

The product is optimized for clarity and repeatability, not maximal feature count.

Signal Construction

How the public pages should be interpreted

Market pulse

Use the cross-market cards for opening context and relative tone, not as a full intraday workflow.

TR funds

The TEFAS layer emphasizes investor growth, allocation drift, and relative ranking to surface regime changes quickly.

BIST catalysts

The KAP layer rewards material disclosure flow, contract density, and capital efficiency. Contract ratios are catalyst signals, not realized revenue promises.

Risk mood

The sentiment badge compresses Fear & Greed and VIX into a readable posture label; it is deliberately simple.

Commercial Model

Why the public build stays narrow

Research-first monetization

FundPilot keeps the public surface sponsor-safe and affiliate-safe instead of bloating the experience with login-heavy consumer features.

Operational discipline

From an investor or fund-manager lens, a narrower, stable product is more credible than a broad but brittle interface.

What to expect next

Further improvements should strengthen data freshness, explicit methodology, and sponsor quality before adding monetization complexity.
